About Us

The Kikuyu Council of Elders is a traditional leadership institution among the Kikuyu people of Kenya. Historically, it has served as a governing body, providing guidance, resolving conflicts, and preserving cultural practices and traditions within the Kikuyu community.

About Us

The Council's leadership has evolved over time, especially in the post-colonial era, where it took on a more active role in social and political issues affecting the Kikuyu community. The Council often provides advice on political matters, advocates for the community's interests, and maintains its cultural heritage.

About Us

Wachira Kiago, as the chairman of the Kikuyu Council of Elders, has been a prominent figure, especially in recent years. He has been instrumental in repositioning the Council's role within contemporary Kenyan society. Under his leadership, the Council has been involved in various peace-building initiatives, community development, and cultural preservation efforts, while also navigating complex political landscapes. The Council, led by Kiago, often voices its opinions on national matters and seeks to influence decisions that affect the Kikuyu community.
